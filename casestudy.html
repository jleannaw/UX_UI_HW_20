Neighborhood App

Overview

UX Case Study: Imagine you've just been hired as a product designer for a new travel startup. The startup’s goal is to build a mobile app to help the modern traveler to plan their next trip.

Programs Utilized: Figma, Miro, InVision, Adobe Creative Suite | Timeline: December 2021 - January 2022

 
 
Step 1
 
 

Overview

The Neighborhood app helps travelers who are concerned about safety, weather, and cost implications when planning their travel.

 
 
Step 2
 
 

Problem

Users are spending a considerable amount of time and energy planning their travel by weighing the safety, weather, and cost factors of various travel locations.

 
 
Step 3
 
 

Our Solution

We believe that including safety resources, weather information, and budget planning features in our product is important for people looking to travel because it empowers them to make better informed decisions, which helps build trust in our products. The Neighborhood app recommends travel places based on the users interests, refines the recommendations based on user needs, and helps them plan their trip once a location has been decided on.

 
 
Step 4
 
 

User Research

As user researchers, we want to understand our user’s thought process about how and why they choose a location to travel to.

  • Objective 1: Discover what motivates users to travel to a certain location.
  • Objective 2: Discover what steps users take when planning their trip.
  • Objective 3: Discover how users interact when they have arrived at their travel location.
We conducted five interviews, transcribed them, and generated a word cloud that lists the top 20 consistent words found across the five interviews we conducted.

 
 
Step 5
 
 

User Flow

This user flow allows the user to input their preferences to ensure a consistent and useful product experience.

 
 
Step 6
 
 

Usability Testing

Our User Testing Plan focused on five specific tasks: sign up for the app, browse the curated list of travel locations, begin creating a trip, continue creating a trip, and complete planning your trip.

  • Plan: The goal and objective of our testing plan was to view if the user was able to sign up and successfully book a trip on our app.
  • Test: We tested five individuals utilizing a virtual testing platform. Our results showed a varied success rate from 75% to 100% depending on the specific task.
  • Iterate: Based on the results of the tests, changes were made to the app to improve the user experience. An analyzation matrix was created to determine low to high priorities for both the user and the developer. Changes that were made included language changes, the addition of more back buttons or "start over" options, and increasing the text size.

 
 
Step 7
 
 

Final Design

The final design of the app incorporates new additions based on the user feedback while staying true to the initial solution based on interview and survey data.
